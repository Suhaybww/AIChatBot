Academic Information Center

Important: All academic policy changes are effective immediately upon publication.

Degree Requirements

All undergraduate students must complete a minimum of 120 credit hours to be eligible for graduation. This includes completion of general education requirements, major-specific coursework, and elective credits.

GPA Requirements

  • Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 required for graduation
  • Major GPA of 2.5 required in most programs
  • Dean's List: 3.5 GPA with 12+ credit hours
  • Academic probation: Below 2.0 cumulative GPA

Course Prerequisites

Students must complete prerequisite courses with a grade of C or better before enrolling in advanced coursework. Placement test scores may substitute for some prerequisites.

Student Services Directory

Registration Services

Course registration opens according to student classification and credit hours completed. Students can add or drop courses during the first two weeks of each semester without penalty.

Financial Aid Office

Complete your FAFSA by March 1st for priority consideration of federal and institutional aid. Payment plans and emergency financial assistance are available through the bursar's office.

Housing and Dining

On-campus housing applications are processed on a first-come, first-served basis. Meal plans are required for all residence hall residents.

Getting Help - Support Resources

Academic Support

The Academic Success Center provides free tutoring services, study groups, and learning workshops. Schedule appointments online or visit during walk-in hours.

Technical Support

IT Help Desk assists with student portal access, email setup, and campus Wi-Fi connectivity. Submit help tickets online or call the support hotline.

Counseling Services

Mental health counseling and wellness programs are available to all enrolled students. Services include individual counseling, group therapy, and crisis intervention.
